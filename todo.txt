cprt:
1. company factors (micro):
  - auction volumes
    - vehicles sold is the lifeblood
      - driven by insurance write-offs (accidents, weather events, & total lossses)
  - take rate/fee growth 
    - higher service fees => margin expansion
  - geographic expansion
    - us core -> growth into germany, uk, spain, & brazil
  - new yard openings = capacity => higher future volumes
  - technology/platform
    - digital auctions, bidding algorithms, ai for damage assessment
    - more buyers from emerging markets => higher demand for salvaged cars
  - balance sheet strength
    - no debt, strong fcfs, large land bank
    - conservative model is prized
2. industry & competitive dynamics (meso):
  - insurance industry trends 
    - coparts main customers are auto insurers (state farm, progressive, & allstate)
    - if insurers declare mroe cars "totaled," CPRT benefits
  - vehicle prices/repair costs
    - high new & used car prices push insurers to total vehicles more often 
    - expensive parts + labor => more salvage volume
  - competition
    - international auto auctions (RBA) is the main competitor
    - market share battles influence margins and volumes
  - regulatory environment 
    - salvage title laws, export restrictions, environmental regulations
3. market-wide forces (macro):
  - auto market health
    - strong demand for used cars boosts resale values of salvage vehicles
    - weak auto market reduces buyer appetite
  - interest rates & credit 
    - higher rates make car loans more expensive => more defaults => more repossesions (extra supply)
  - weather & catastrophes
    - hurricanes, floods, & hailstorms => spike in total-loss claims
  - global trade
    - salvage buyers export cars to nigeria, e. europe, & la
    - strong usd => weaker demand abroad
4. market structure/flow factors:
  - index fund in/outflows directly impact demand (s&p 500)
  - growth stock characteristics
    - trades at a premium multiple due to steady growth and moat
    - if growth/momentum etfs are sold, copart drops with them
  - short interest
    - low, but some traders short on valuation concerns
  - liquidity & volatility
    - cprt has less volume, so block trades can move it sharply
5. exogenous shocks:
  - natural disasters
  - supply chain/auto manufacturing 
  - insurance regulation
    - "total losses" classification
  - global political risks
